# Texture & Finish

Guidance for achieving print-like Art Nouveau finishes in digital or physical output.

## 1) Lithographic Grain
- Simulate fine stochastic grain rather than uniform digital noise.
- Grain intensity target:
  - Highlights: 2–4%
  - Midtones: 4–8%
  - Shadows: 6–10%
- Keep grain scale consistent across the piece; sudden scale shifts look synthetic.

## 2) Paper Tone Selection
- Recommended base tones:
  - Warm cream: `#F2E8D5`
  - Aged ivory: `#E8DABD`
  - Cool rag: `#E3E5DE`
- Avoid pure white backgrounds unless intentionally modernizing the treatment.
- Slightly darken paper tone near outer margins to suggest age and handling.

## 3) Ink Edge Behavior
- Emulate minor edge spread where dark ink meets absorbent stock.
- Keep edge irregularity subtle (roughly 0.2–0.6 px in screen workflows).
- On high-contrast outlines, apply occasional micro-breaks to mimic plate wear.

## 4) Registration Drift (Optional Vintage Effect)
- Offset secondary color pass by a tiny amount (0.2–0.8 px) in selected regions.
- Restrict drift to accents or shadow plates; global drift harms readability.
- Use in moderation to avoid a novelty/comic look.

## 5) Halftone and Solid Fill Balance
- Pair smooth solids with halftone or stippled transitions, especially in gradients.
- Avoid fully smooth vector gradients for large atmospheric fields; introduce tactile texture.
- Maintain clear silhouette edges even when internal texture is complex.

## 6) Finish Presets

### Clean Atelier
- Low grain, crisp edges, minimal drift.
- Best for contemporary reinterpretations and brand systems.

### Archive Poster
- Medium grain, warm paper tone, gentle edge spread.
- Best for historical homage and collectible print aesthetics.

### Antique Ephemera
- Higher grain, visible edge wear, localized discoloration.
- Best for prop design, narrative worldbuilding, and faux-found artifacts.

## 7) Output Checks
- Zoom to 100% and 200% to verify texture does not overpower line hierarchy.
- Print a small proof to validate paper-tone assumptions under real lighting.
- Ensure text remains legible against grain and tonal variation before final export.
