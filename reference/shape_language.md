# Shape Language

Defines geometry and line principles for coherent Art Nouveau styling.

## 1) Line Curvature Rules
- Prefer **whiplash curves** with long acceleration and gentle deceleration.
- Avoid abrupt angle changes unless marking a structural break (frame corners, sigils).
- Maintain line family consistency: if primary stems are thick-to-thin tapered, secondary lines should echo the same taper ratio.
- Recommended stroke hierarchy:
  - Primary contour: 1.0x
  - Secondary connective: 0.6x
  - Micro ornament: 0.35x

## 2) Border Geometry
- Use nested borders in three bands:
  1. **Outer restraint line** (thin, quiet)
  2. **Ornamental band** (motif repeat)
  3. **Inner breathing margin** (minimal detail)
- Corner logic options:
  - **Botanical corners:** mirrored leaf scrolls.
  - **Architectural corners:** chamfer + bead chain.
  - **Celestial corners:** quarter medallion with star rays.
- Keep border thickness between 8% and 15% of total composition width.

## 3) Medallion Patterns
- Preferred medallion archetypes:
  - **Solar rosette:** concentric petals around a circular core.
  - **Cameo oval:** portrait or emblem framed by pearl dots.
  - **Hex floral wheel:** six-petal rotational symmetry for balanced panels.
- Place medallion centers on major alignment axes (vertical midpoint, rule-of-thirds intersections).
- Use radial echoes (rings, dots, mini petals) to tie medallions back to surrounding motifs.

## 4) Ornament Density Tiers

### Tier I — Sparse
- Coverage: ~10–20% ornamented area.
- Use for editorial or typographic clarity.
- Prioritize breathing room and one dominant flourish.

### Tier II — Balanced
- Coverage: ~25–40% ornamented area.
- Recommended default for posters and illustrated title pages.
- Mix one major motif stream with one secondary repeat.

### Tier III — Opulent
- Coverage: ~45–65% ornamented area.
- Suitable for ceremonial prints and exhibition pieces.
- Requires strict value control so detail does not collapse into noise.

### Tier IV — Maximal Relic
- Coverage: ~70–85% ornamented area.
- Use sparingly for decorative plates, not body-text-heavy layouts.
- Introduce deliberate low-detail pockets every 1/6th of width to preserve legibility.

## 5) Composition Continuity Checklist
- Curves lead eye in a loop, not dead ends.
- Motif scale transitions are gradual across adjacent zones.
- Negative space forms recognizable silhouettes, not accidental voids.
- At least one geometric anchor (medallion, cartouche, frame) stabilizes organic flow.
