# Art Nouveau: Bibliography

## Overview

This bibliography catalogues the principal scholarly and popular resources for the study of Art Nouveau — its history, its regional expressions, its individual practitioners, its techniques, and its contemporary applications. Resources are organised by category.

---

## 1. General Art Nouveau Surveys

### Foundational Academic Studies

**Madsen, Stephan Tschudi.** *Sources of Art Nouveau.* Oslo: H. Aschehoug, 1956. English translation, New York: Wittenborn, 1956.  
The first serious academic study of the movement's origins and international expressions. Madsen's analytical approach established Art Nouveau as a legitimate subject of art-historical inquiry and remains an essential starting point.

**Schmutzler, Robert.** *Art Nouveau / Jugendstil.* Stuttgart: Hatje, 1962. English translation, New York: Abrams, 1964.  
The first comprehensive illustrated survey of the movement in its full international scope. Schmutzler's 322-page text, with hundreds of illustrations, introduced a generation of readers outside academia to the movement's range and ambition. Still in print.

**Selz, Peter, and Mildred Constantine (eds).** *Art Nouveau: Art and Design at the Turn of the Century.* New York: Museum of Modern Art, 1959.  
The catalogue of the MoMA exhibition that rehabilitated Art Nouveau for a postwar audience. Essays by major scholars; the most influential single publication in the movement's postwar reception history.

**Rheims, Maurice.** *The Age of Art Nouveau.* London: Thames and Hudson, 1966. (French original: *L'Art 1900.* Paris: Arts et Métiers Graphiques, 1965.)  
Comprehensive survey with particular attention to the decorative arts and luxury objects.

**Greenhalgh, Paul (ed).** *Art Nouveau: 1890–1914.* London: V&A Publications, 2000.  
Catalogue of the V&A's centennial exhibition of 2000; excellent essays on all regional variants and media, with superb colour illustrations.

**Duncan, Alastair.** *Art Nouveau.* London: Thames and Hudson, 1994. (World of Art series.)  
Accessible, well-illustrated introduction to the movement; recommended as a starting survey for newcomers to the field.

## 2. Alphonse Mucha: Monographs and Studies

**Mucha, Jiří.** *Alphonse Mucha: His Life and Art.* London: Heinemann, 1966.  
The first comprehensive monograph, written by the artist's son. Invaluable for biographical detail and access to primary sources, though somewhat uncritical in its assessments.

**Rennert, Jack, and Alain Weill.** *Alphonse Mucha: The Complete Posters and Panels.* New York: Harmony Books, 1984.  
The complete catalogue raisonné of Mucha's commercial poster and panel work. Essential reference for identification, dating, and provenance.

**Mucha, Sarah.** *Alphonse Mucha.* London: Frances Lincoln, 2005.  
A beautifully illustrated overview published by the Mucha Foundation; authoritative on the documentary record and particularly strong on the *Slav Epic*.

**Bridges, Ann.** *Alphonse Mucha: The Complete Graphic Works.* London: Academy Editions, 1980.  
Comprehensive survey of the full graphic output.

**Documents Décoratifs.** Paris: Librairie Centrale des Beaux-Arts, 1902. (Facsimile edition, New York: Dover, 1981.)  
Mucha's own portfolio of design resources — borders, figures, furniture designs — intended as a practical design reference. Still the most direct source for understanding his ornamental vocabulary.

## 3. Gustav Klimt and the Vienna Secession

**Breicha, Otto (ed).** *Ver Sacrum.* Vienna: Jugend und Volk, 1972.  
Facsimile selections from the Secession journal; the only way to encounter the complete integrated design of *Ver Sacrum* without access to an archive holding original issues.

**Fliedl, Gottfried.** *Klimt.* Cologne: Taschen, 1992. (Multiple editions.)  
Standard accessible monograph; good illustration quality.

**Kallir, Jane.** *Gustav Klimt: 200 Drawings.* New York: Harry N. Abrams, 2012.  
The drawings archive is essential for understanding Klimt's working method; his preparatory sketches demonstrate the relationship between his decorative and figurative impulses.

**Néret, Gilles.** *Klimt.* Cologne: Taschen, 1993.  
Accessible Taschen survey with excellent reproductions.

**Vergo, Peter.** *Art in Vienna: Klimt, Kokoschka, Schiele and Their Contemporaries.* London: Phaidon, 1975.  
Essential contextual study; places Klimt within the broader Viennese cultural and artistic world of the Secession era.

**Brandstätter, Christian (ed).** *Vienna 1900: Art, Life and Culture.* New York: Vendome Press, 2006.  
Comprehensive illustrated survey of the Vienna 1900 cultural world.

## 4. Regional Studies

### Belgium and France

**Borsi, Franco, and Henri Ranieri.** *Brussels 1900: The Capital of Art Nouveau.* Brussels: Mardaga, 1991.  
Essential survey of the Brussels variant, with detailed architectural coverage of Horta and his contemporaries.

**Duncan, Alastair.** *Paris Salons, 1895–1914.* 5 vols. Woodbridge: Antique Collectors' Club, 1994.  
Comprehensive documentary survey of Art Nouveau applied arts exhibitions in Paris, including furniture, metalwork, and ceramics.

**Thiébaut, Philippe.** *Guimard.* Paris: Réunion des musées nationaux, 1992.  
Catalogue of the major retrospective of Guimard's work; the definitive reference on his architecture and applied arts.

### Germany and Austria

**Howarth, Thomas.** *Charles Rennie Mackintosh and the Modern Movement.* London: Routledge, 1952.  
The standard academic study of Mackintosh's work and its relationship to the Vienna Secession.

**Houze, Rebecca.** *Textiles, Fashion, and Design Reform in Austria-Hungary Before the First World War.* Farnham: Ashgate, 2015.  
Scholarly study of the Wiener Werkstätte textiles; excellent on design theory.

**Barron, Stephanie, and Wolf Tegethoff (eds).** *1900: Art at the Crossroads.* London: Royal Academy of Arts, 2000.  
Major exhibition catalogue focusing on the transition between Art Nouveau and early modernism.

### Glasgow Style

**Billcliffe, Roger.** *Charles Rennie Mackintosh: The Complete Furniture, Furniture Drawings and Interior Designs.* 4th edn. Guilford: Lutterworth Press, 2009.  
The catalogue raisonné of Mackintosh's furniture and interior design work.

**Neat, Timothy.** *Part Seen, Part Imagined: Meaning and Symbolism in the Work of Charles Rennie Mackintosh and Margaret Macdonald.* Edinburgh: Canongate, 1994.  
Interpretive study of the symbolic content of the Glasgow Four's work.

### Spain / Catalonia

**Bassegoda Nonell, Joan.** *Antonio Gaudí: Master Architect.* New York: Abbeville Press, 2000.  
Standard comprehensive monograph on Gaudí's architecture.

**van Hensbergen, Gijs.** *Gaudí: The Biography.* London: HarperCollins, 2001.  
Accessible and well-researched biography; particularly strong on the Catalan cultural and political context.

## 5. Technique-Focused Studies

### Glass

**Koch, Robert.** *Louis C. Tiffany: Rebel in Glass.* 3rd edn. New York: Crown Publishers, 1982.  
The standard monograph on Tiffany's glass work; well-illustrated.

**Arwas, Victor.** *Glass: Art Nouveau to Art Deco.* London: Academy Editions, 1977.  
Survey of European and American decorative glass across the transition period.

**Hilschenz-Mlynek, Helga, and Helmut Ricke.** *Glas: Historismus, Jugendstil, Art Déco.* Munich: Prestel, 1985.  
German-language but extensively illustrated; the standard reference for Nancy School and European glass.

### Jewellery

**Becker, Vivienne.** *Art Nouveau Jewellery.* London: Thames and Hudson, 1985.  
The standard survey of Art Nouveau jewellery; covers all major practitioners and techniques.

**Vever, Henri.** *La bijouterie française au XIXe siècle.* 3 vols. Paris: Floury, 1906–1908. (Facsimile edition, Florence: SPES, 1991.)  
Vever was himself a major Art Nouveau jeweller; his three-volume history of nineteenth-century French jewellery, published during the Art Nouveau era, is an invaluable primary source.

### Posters

**Weill, Alain.** *The Poster: A Worldwide Survey and History.* Paris: G. K. Hall, 1985.  
Comprehensive history of poster art from the 1860s through the mid-twentieth century.

**Devynck, Danièle, and Danièle Heilpern.** *Toulouse-Lautrec.* Cologne: Taschen, 2004.  
Standard accessible survey of Toulouse-Lautrec's poster work; excellent reproductions.

---

## 6. The Psychedelic Poster Revival

**Grushkin, Paul.** *The Art of Rock: Posters from Presley to Punk.* New York: Abbeville Press, 1987.  
The standard reference for the rock concert poster tradition, including the San Francisco psychedelic artists.

**Moscoso, Victor.** *Sex, Rock & Optical Illusions.* Seattle: Fantagraphics, 2006.  
Collected poster work by one of the Art Nouveau revival's most technically accomplished practitioners; includes commentary by Moscoso on his methods and sources.

---

## 7. Exhibition Catalogues (Selected Major Exhibitions)

**Lahor, Jean.** *L'Art Nouveau.* Paris: H. Laurens, 1901.  
Contemporary document; one of the first books to survey the new movement and give it a critical framework.

**Greenhalgh, Paul (ed).** *Art Nouveau: 1890–1914.* London: V&A Publications, 2000. *(Listed above; repeated for emphasis as the essential centennial survey.)*

**Dufrène, Florent (ed).** *Art Nouveau en Europe.* Paris: Éditions du Patrimoine, 1999.  
French survey for the centennial; useful on the geographical spread of the movement.
