# Motif Dictionary

This reference defines canonical Art Nouveau motifs, their symbolic meaning, recommended placement, and compatible pairings.

## 1) Iris (Fleur-de-Lis Family)
- **Semantic meaning:** Wisdom, message-bearing, transition between worlds.
- **Placement guidance:** Vertical accents near side borders, column-like separators, and lower-corner blooms that lead the eye upward.
- **Best pairings:** Dragonfly wings, whip-vine stems, moon discs.

## 2) Poppy
- **Semantic meaning:** Sleep, memory, reverie, fragile beauty.
- **Placement guidance:** Mid-to-lower field clusters, especially where a composition needs softness after dense linework.
- **Best pairings:** Curled tendrils, crescent moon, muted nocturne palettes.

## 3) Lily
- **Semantic meaning:** Purity, renewal, ceremonial elegance.
- **Placement guidance:** Central medallions, altar-like vertical compositions, or as mirrored pairs bracketing a title panel.
- **Best pairings:** Gold filigree, pearl beads, pale green stems.

## 4) Rose
- **Semantic meaning:** Devotion, secrecy, sensuality.
- **Placement guidance:** Circular frames, wreath borders, cameo medallions, and shoulder-height focal anchors in figure posters.
- **Best pairings:** Thorn scrollwork, ribbon banners, warm dawn palettes.

## 5) Ginkgo Leaf
- **Semantic meaning:** Longevity, resilience, memory over time.
- **Placement guidance:** Fan-like corner terminators, repeated frieze motifs, and geometricized border transitions.
- **Best pairings:** Radial sunbursts, coin medallions, restrained geometry.

## 6) Water Lily
- **Semantic meaning:** Stillness, reflection, emergence from depth.
- **Placement guidance:** Horizontal base bands, mirrored lower thirds, and waterline horizon scenes.
- **Best pairings:** Ripple curves, swan neck forms, cool spring palettes.

## 7) Dragonfly
- **Semantic meaning:** Ephemerality, transformation, summer air.
- **Placement guidance:** Hovering connectors between title and figure, upper-corner diagonals, and repeated wing tiles in borders.
- **Best pairings:** Iris, reeds, translucent enamel-like highlights.

## 8) Peacock Feather / Eye Motif
- **Semantic meaning:** Vision, pride, theatrical opulence.
- **Placement guidance:** Halo backgrounds, fan spreads, and crest-like crown shapes above primary subjects.
- **Best pairings:** Jewel tones, arabesque borders, high-density ornament tiers.

## 9) Vine / Whiplash Tendril
- **Semantic meaning:** Vital energy, growth, continuous motion.
- **Placement guidance:** Primary line engine; use to connect all major elements and avoid isolated islands.
- **Best pairings:** Almost all floral motifs; especially rose, poppy, and grapes.

## 10) Swan Neck Curve
- **Semantic meaning:** Grace, romantic melancholy, noble restraint.
- **Placement guidance:** S-curve transitions in profiles, necklines, arch supports, and basin-like lower frames.
- **Best pairings:** Water lily, moon disc, soft monochrome backgrounds.

## 11) Crescent Moon / Solar Disc
- **Semantic meaning:** Cycles, femininity/masculinity duality, time passage.
- **Placement guidance:** Upper-third celestial anchors, behind-head halos, and nested medallion centers.
- **Best pairings:** Poppy for nocturne, ginkgo for daylight, star-point tessellation.

## 12) Bee / Honeycomb Fragment
- **Semantic meaning:** Industry, collective harmony, sweetness through labor.
- **Placement guidance:** Micro-motifs for trim, garment borders, and secondary pattern fields.
- **Best pairings:** Clover, wildflower sprays, amber-gold accents.

## Placement Heuristics (Quick Rules)
- Keep **1 dominant motif family**, **1 support family**, and **1 micro-detail family** per composition.
- Avoid equal visual weight across all motifs; prioritize hierarchy: focal > connective > textural.
- When using symbolic dualities (sun/moon, bloom/seed), separate them by vertical levels to preserve clarity.
- Repeat motif silhouettes in at least three places to create intentional rhythm.
