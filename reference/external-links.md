# Art Nouveau: External Links and Online Resources

## Overview

This document provides an annotated directory of online resources for Art Nouveau research, image reference, and contemporary practice. Resources are organised by type and focus.

---

## 1. Museums with Major Collections

### Musée d'Orsay, Paris
**URL:** https://www.musee-orsay.fr  
**Collection focus:** French Art Nouveau applied arts; Guimard architectural elements; major Toulouse-Lautrec and poster collections; Nancy School glass and furniture; Lalique jewellery  
**Online access:** Extensive searchable database with many high-quality images. The museum's collections database (*Œuvres* section) provides access to thousands of Art Nouveau works.  
**Note:** The Musée d'Orsay holds the most comprehensive single collection of French Art Nouveau applied arts in the world, including major Gallé glass, Majorelle furniture, and Mucha decorative panels.

### Mucha Museum, Prague
**URL:** https://www.mucha.cz  
**Collection focus:** Alphonse Mucha: posters, decorative panels, drawings, personal objects, *Slav Epic* documentation  
**Online access:** Website includes collection overview, biographical resources, and exhibition information. The *Slav Epic* itself is on extended loan to the National Gallery Prague (Veletržní palác).  
**Note:** The Mucha Museum in Prague's Panská Street is the primary public repository of Mucha's commercial and decorative work. The Mucha Foundation website provides additional biographical and scholarly resources.

### Österreichische Galerie Belvedere, Vienna
**URL:** https://www.belvedere.at  
**Collection focus:** Gustav Klimt: *The Kiss*, *Judith I*, *Judith II*, and other major works; Vienna Secession material  
**Online access:** Collection database with high-quality reproductions; *The Kiss* and other key works are available at high resolution.  
**Note:** The Belvedere holds the single most important collection of Klimt's paintings and is the essential reference for his mature work.

### Vienna Secession Building
**URL:** https://www.secession.at  
**Collection focus:** The building itself; Klimt's *Beethoven Frieze* (permanent display in the basement gallery)  
**Note:** The *Beethoven Frieze* is on permanent display and cannot be moved; visiting the Secession building is the only way to see it. The building continues to function as a contemporary art exhibition space.

### Tiffany Studios Collection — Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York
**URL:** https://www.metmuseum.org  
**Collection focus:** Major Tiffany Studios works; leaded glass lamps, windows, and Favrile glass  
**Online access:** MetCollections database provides high-resolution images of most collection items.  
**Note:** The Met holds the most important American collection of Tiffany Studios work; the American Wing permanent galleries include major examples.

### Corning Museum of Glass, New York
**URL:** https://www.cmog.org  
**Collection focus:** International studio glass including major Art Nouveau glass — Gallé, Daum, Tiffany; comprehensive glass history collection  
**Online access:** Collections database with images; extensive educational resources on glass-making techniques.  
**Note:** The definitive museum resource for Art Nouveau glass technique; the educational materials on cameo glass, favrile glass, and plique-à-jour are excellent.

### Victoria and Albert Museum, London
**URL:** https://www.vam.ac.uk  
**Collection focus:** Comprehensive Art Nouveau applied arts across all media; strong holdings in British (Beardsley, Mackintosh), Continental, and American material  
**Online access:** V&A Collections database with excellent search functionality and high-quality images; most items photographed.  
**Note:** The V&A's Art Nouveau holdings are among the most comprehensive outside Paris, with particular strengths in jewellery, glass, and textiles.

### Musée Horta, Brussels
**URL:** https://www.hortamuseum.be  
**Collection focus:** The architect's own house (1898–1901); complete surviving Art Nouveau interior with original furniture, fittings, and decorative arts  
**Note:** The most important surviving Art Nouveau interior in the world; seeing the Musée Horta is essential for understanding the Gesamtkunstwerk in architecture. UNESCO World Heritage Site.

### Museum of Decorative Arts (Musée des Arts Décoratifs), Paris
**URL:** https://madparis.fr  
**Collection focus:** Comprehensive French decorative arts; extensive Art Nouveau holdings in furniture, glass, ceramics, jewellery, and poster art  
**Note:** Holdings include major Nancy School furniture and glass, Lalique jewellery, and Mucha print material.

## 2. Online Image Archives and Databases

### Wikimedia Commons: Art Nouveau Category
**URL:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Art_Nouveau  
Extensive and well-organised repository of public domain images of Art Nouveau works. Subcategories by medium, nationality, and artist provide systematic access. Image quality varies; major works are typically available at high resolution.

### Europeana: Art Nouveau Collections
**URL:** https://www.europeana.eu  
Aggregated European museum digital collections; search "Art Nouveau" or specific artist names to access images from dozens of European museum collections simultaneously.

### The Art Institute of Chicago Digital Collections
**URL:** https://www.artic.edu/collection  
Comprehensive digital access to the museum's holdings; notable Art Nouveau poster collection including major Mucha and Toulouse-Lautrec works at high resolution.

### Smithsonian Open Access
**URL:** https://www.si.edu/openaccess  
High-resolution images of Smithsonian collection items available for free download and use; includes Art Nouveau material from the Smithsonian's multiple constituent museums.

### New York Public Library Digital Collections
**URL:** https://digitalcollections.nypl.org  
Extensive poster and print collections including major Art Nouveau holdings; many items available at very high resolution.

### Princeton University Art Museum Digital Collection
**URL:** https://artmuseum.princeton.edu/collections  
Strong holdings in Art Nouveau graphic work; comprehensive online access.

## 3. Architectural Resources

### Institut National du Patrimoine (France)
**URL:** https://www.inp.fr  
Resources on French heritage buildings including Guimard's Métro structures and other protected Art Nouveau buildings.

### UNESCO World Heritage — Art Nouveau Sites
**URL:** https://whc.unesco.org  
Search for "Art Nouveau" or individual building names to access official UNESCO documentation of the Horta town houses and other protected sites. Documentation includes architectural analysis, conservation reports, and high-quality photography.

### Architekturzentrum Wien (Architectural Centre Vienna)
**URL:** https://www.azw.at  
Comprehensive online archive of Viennese architectural history, including the Vienna Secession and Wagner's work on the Stadtbahn. German-language; good image resources.

## 4. Scholarly and Research Resources

### JSTOR Art History Resources
**URL:** https://www.jstor.org  
Academic articles on Art Nouveau from major art history journals. Searches for "Art Nouveau," "Jugendstil," "Vienna Secession," "Mucha," and individual artist names yield extensive scholarly literature. Many articles freely accessible.

### Grove Art Online / Oxford Art Online
**URL:** https://www.oxfordartonline.com  
Subscription required; the most comprehensive and authoritative online art encyclopaedia. Art Nouveau entries and individual artist entries are peer-reviewed and regularly updated.

### The Art Nouveau Network
**URL:** https://www.artnouveau-net.eu  
European network for the preservation and promotion of Art Nouveau cultural heritage; resources on architecture, applied arts, and cultural tourism across European Art Nouveau sites.

---

## 5. Typography Resources

### Klingspor Museum, Offenbach am Main, Germany
**URL:** https://www.klingspor-museum.de  
The Klingspor Museum holds the archive of the Klingspor type foundry, which published the Eckmann typeface (1900) and other Jugendstil typefaces. Online resources include historical specimen books and typographic documentation.

### Letterform Archive, San Francisco
**URL:** https://letterformarchive.org  
Extensive collection of historical typography, including Art Nouveau and Jugendstil printed material; some online access, with extensive in-person research facilities.

---

## 6. Contemporary Practice Resources

### Society of Decorative Painters
**URL:** https://decorativepainters.com  
Resources for decorative painting practitioners, including botanical and ornamental styles with Art Nouveau influences.

### The Handmade Jeweller (UK)
**URL:** https://www.thehandmadejeweller.com  
Resources for studio jewellery, including enamel techniques (plique-à-jour, cloisonné) of the type used in Art Nouveau jewellery.

### Procreate Community / ArtStation Community
**URL:** https://procreate.com/community and https://www.artstation.com  
Contemporary digital illustration communities where Neo-Art Nouveau work circulates and where practitioners share technique information. Search for "Art Nouveau illustration" or "Neo Art Nouveau" to find active communities.

---

## 7. Periodicals and Journals

### *Journal of Decorative and Propaganda Arts*
Published by the Wolfson Foundation; scholarly articles on decorative arts history including Art Nouveau.

### *Nineteenth-Century Art Worldwide*
**URL:** https://www.19thc-artworldwide.org  
Open-access online journal; frequent articles on Art Nouveau.

### *The Burlington Magazine*
The leading English-language peer-reviewed journal of art history; Art Nouveau appears regularly in the historical coverage.
